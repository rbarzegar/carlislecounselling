Carlisle School Counselling is a collaboration of experienced counsellors working in primary and secondary schools within Carlisle and Cumbria.

For more information on how your school can access our counselling support, please contact us: info@carlisleschoolcounselling.co.uk

Benefits of counselling

Evidence indicates that counselling in schools can underpin academic achievement, improve behaviour and concentration as well as improving the general well-being of children and young people.


The counselling sessions are around 45 minutes long and progress will be reviewed after 6 sessions. We welcome feedback from staff, parents and of course the children we work with at any time. We trust this will be a rewarding experience for staff, children and parents.

Why have a Counsellor in School?

A school based Counsellor is able to see children in a place that is familiar, secure and convenient. If children receive emotional support from a qualified professional they will have greater opportunity to fulfill their potential. Some issues that may affect children where counselling could help:

  • Divorce or separation of parents
  • Bereavement
  • Parent in prison
  • Peer problems in school
  • Having experienced trauma through neglect, violence or abuse
  • Being bullied or bulling someone else

If you are a parent/carer enquiring about counselling for your child, all referrals must be made through your child's school.

Our services

We provide the following service(s):

  • One-to-one counselling for pupils in primary and secondary education.
  • Staff Counselling
  • Staff Supervision Groups (see notes below)
  • Primary-to-Secondary transition course.

Carlisle School Counselling also offers a Staff Support Service for those in Senior Safeguarding roles as well as personal counselling for any member of staff.

External supervision has proven to be an effective way of supporting staff employed in senior safeguarding and emotionally demanding roles and can be of great benefit to individuals or groups of staff.

Two or our team, Jeanette Carson and Anna Lythgoe, are accredited and experienced Supervisors, who have worked with members of staff in the NHS, Charity and Education sectors.


They provide a source of support and professional development for those attending Staff Supervision Groups from schools in Cumbria and offer a safe and professional space for staff to explore their challenging roles and to network with other professionals in the area.


Colleagues who attend the group sessions are usually Safeguarding Leads or pastoral workers who are deeply immersed in welfare and emotional well-being issues at their schools

Our Team

All of our counsellors are registered members of the British Association for Counselling & Psychotherapy (BACP) and hold a formal BACP accredited qualification in counselling, offering a range of appropriate experience. We all receive monthly clinical supervision in accordance with the BACP guidelines.

All counsellors hold an up to date and satisfactory Disclosure and Barring Service certificate (enhanced level) and individual professional indemnity insurance.

Each counsellor either holds, or is working towards, individual professional accreditation with the BACP.

Our role as Counsellors

We see our role as one of supporting and enhancing what you are already doing in school. Counselling offers a safe space in which children can explore confusing or upsetting feelings and behaviours. The therapeutic play and active listening in which we engage, allows children to express themselves and tell their story.
